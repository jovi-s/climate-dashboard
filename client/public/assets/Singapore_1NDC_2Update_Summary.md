**Singapore's Updated 2030 Climate Target (NDC):**

*   Singapore has updated its first NDC, committing to reduce its greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions to **around 60 million tonnes of carbon dioxide equivalent (MtCO2e) in 2030**, after achieving peak emissions earlier.
*   This is an **economy-wide, absolute emissions limitation target** for the single year 2030, covering all key sectors (Energy, Industrial Processes and Product Use, Agriculture, LULUCF, Waste) and major GHGs (CO2, CH4, N2O, HFCs, PFCs, SF6, NF3).
*   The implementation period is **2021 to 2030**.

**Methodology and Accounting:**

*   Singapore will account for its emissions and removals using the **2006 IPCC Guidelines** (and subsequent supplements/guidance), employing a Sectoral approach.
*   GHG emissions will be aggregated using **100-year Global Warming Potential (GWP) values from the IPCC Fifth Assessment Report**.
*   Progress will be demonstrated through national GHG inventory time series reported in its Biennial Transparency Reports.

**Planning and Implementation:**

*   The NDC was developed considering national circumstances, stakeholder consultations, and technical studies. The **Inter-Ministerial Committee on Climate Change (IMCCC)** coordinates efforts.
*   Key national strategies include the **Singapore Green Plan 2030**.
*   Implementation relies on domestic measures, including a **carbon tax** (set to rise significantly by 2030), energy efficiency improvements, solar deployment, and sector-specific policies (transport, buildings, industry).
*   Singapore may utilize **international cooperation under Article 6 of the Paris Agreement (e.g., ITMOs)** to help achieve its target, ensuring environmental integrity.

**Fairness, Ambition, and National Context:**

*   The updated target is presented as more ambitious, responding to the Glasgow Climate Pact and reflecting Singapore's commitment despite national constraints.
*   Key considerations influencing fairness and ambition include:
    *   **Early Actions:** Past policies like switching to natural gas for power generation and implementing vehicle quotas.
    *   **Constraints:** Being alternative energy-disadvantaged (limited land, low renewable potential), high population density limiting solar/carbon sinks, dependence on global supply chains, and vulnerability to climate change requiring significant adaptation.
    *   **Strengths:** High carbon intensity performance globally, strong focus on energy efficiency, active promotion of solar PV deployment despite limits, and robust implementation efforts like the carbon tax.
    *   **International Role:** Active participation in international climate cooperation and capacity building for developing countries.

**Contribution to Global Goals:**

*   The 2030 NDC target, combined with Singapore's stated ambition to achieve **net-zero emissions by 2050**, is framed as contributing towards the long-term temperature goal of the Paris Agreement and the overall objective of the UNFCCC.

**Adaptation:**

*   Comprehensive adaptation efforts are underway to address climate vulnerabilities, particularly as a low-lying island state. Detailed information is available in Singapore's separate Adaptation Communication.
