## Comprehensive Summary of Singapore’s Second Update to its First Nationally Determined Contribution (NDC)

Singapore’s Second Update to its First Nationally Determined Contribution (NDC) under the Paris Agreement reaffirms and strengthens its commitment to global climate action. The key highlights of this NDC include:

### Ambitious 2030 Target and Early Emissions Peak
- **Headline Target:** Reduce national emissions to around 60 million tonnes of CO₂ equivalent (MtCO₂e) by 2030.
- **Peaking Emissions:** Aims to peak emissions before 2030, signaling urgency and ambition beyond a single-year target.
- **Implementation Period:** While the formal NDC period spans 2021–2030, the focus is on achieving the quantifiable target in 2030 and ensuring emissions start declining earlier.

### Economy-Wide Scope and Absolute Limitation
- **Coverage:** This economy-wide, absolute GHG emissions limitation includes key sectors—energy, industrial processes, product use, agriculture, land use (LULUCF), and waste.
- **Greenhouse Gases:** Targets all major GHGs, such as carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide, and fluorinated gases, for a comprehensive approach.
- **Fair and Ambitious:** Singapore underscores the target’s ambition, especially given its limited land, high population density, and sparse renewable energy options.

### Strong Institutional Framework
- **Inter-Ministerial Committee on Climate Change (IMCCC):** A high-level, whole-of-government body guiding policy coordination and climate action.
- **Stakeholder Engagement:** Development of the NDC involved consultations with multiple sectors, reflecting a collaborative approach to policymaking.

### Transparent Reporting and Accountability
- **Biennial Transparency Reports (BTRs):** Singapore commits to demonstrating progress through these reports, in line with the Paris Agreement’s enhanced transparency framework.
- **Methodological Rigor:** Will utilize the 2006 IPCC Guidelines for National Greenhouse Gas Inventories, adopting higher-tier methods when data allows.

### Contextualized Ambition Under Unique Constraints
- **National Circumstances:** Singapore highlights its small size, high density, and limited natural resources that restrict domestic renewable energy potential.
- **Early Actions:** Previous steps—fuel switching, a vehicle quota system, and early carbon mitigation measures—lay the foundation for current and future efforts.

### Carbon Tax as a Key Policy Instrument
- **First in Southeast Asia:** Singapore’s pioneering carbon tax covers 80% of its emissions, demonstrating strong policy resolve.
- **Progressive Increase:** Plans to raise the carbon tax from current levels to S$50–S$80 per tonne of emissions by 2030, reinforcing the country’s decarbonization drive.

### Emphasis on International Cooperation
- **Resource Constraints:** Recognizes the importance of international collaboration for clean energy technologies, regional power grids, and carbon storage solutions.
- **Article 6 Mechanisms:** Signals readiness to engage in voluntary cooperation to meet ambitious emission reduction goals.

### Long-Term Vision for Net-Zero by 2050
- **Beyond 2030:** This NDC is part of a broader strategy to achieve net-zero emissions by 2050, aligning with global climate objectives.
- **Sustainable Future:** Reflects Singapore’s commitment to responsible resource stewardship and a substantial contribution to the Paris Agreement’s goals.

By combining a clear 2030 emissions cap, an earlier-than-anticipated peak, economy-wide coverage, robust institutional support, transparent reporting, and a progressive carbon tax, Singapore’s Second Update to its First NDC articulates a determined pathway toward its longer-term vision of net-zero emissions by mid-century. Despite constraints posed by geography and resource limitations, Singapore underscores that comprehensive action, innovation, and global partnerships are integral to its pursuit of ambitious climate goals.
