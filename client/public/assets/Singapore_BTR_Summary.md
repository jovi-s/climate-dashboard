**Overview:**

*   This is Singapore's first Biennial Transparency Report (BTR) submitted under the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC), in accordance with the Enhanced Transparency Framework (ETF) modalities, procedures, and guidelines (MPGs).
*   It covers Singapore's national greenhouse gas (GHG) inventory from 2000 to 2022, tracks progress towards its Nationally Determined Contribution (NDC) under the Paris Agreement, details mitigation actions, provides emissions projections up to 2030, and outlines capacity-building support provided.

**National GHG Inventory (Chapter 1):**

*   Singapore's total net GHG emissions in **2022 were 58.59 million tonnes of CO2 equivalent (MtCO2e)**, including Land Use, Land-Use Change and Forestry (LULUCF).
*   **CO2** is the dominant GHG, accounting for **86%** of total emissions in 2022. Non-CO2 gases (CH4, N2O, HFCs, PFCs, SF6, NF3) make up the remaining 14%. HFC emissions from the Refrigeration and Air-conditioning (RAC) sector are newly included.
*   The **Energy sector** is the largest contributor, primarily due to fuel combustion for power generation and industry. The **Industrial Processes and Product Use (IPPU)** sector is the second largest, mainly due to F-gas emissions. Waste sector emissions are relatively small, LULUCF was a small net source in 2022, and Agriculture emissions are negligible.
*   Inventory methodology follows the **2006 IPCC Guidelines**, primarily Tier 1 but using higher tiers where data is available (e.g., specific industries, LULUCF). Global Warming Potentials (GWPs) from the **IPCC Fifth Assessment Report (AR5)** are used.

**Tracking Progress towards NDC (Chapter 2):**

*   Singapore's **2030 NDC** is to reduce emissions to around **60 MtCO2e** after peaking emissions earlier. This is an economy-wide, absolute limitation target for the year 2030.
*   Based on 2022 emissions (58.59 MtCO2e), Singapore is **on track** to meet its 2030 NDC.
*   **Projections ('With Measures' scenario):** Emissions are projected to peak around **2028** at approximately **61.9 MtCO2e** (including expected ITMO use) / **64.4 MtCO2e** (excluding ITMOs). The 2030 projection is **~60 MtCO2e** (including expected ITMO use) / **~62.5 MtCO2e** (excluding ITMOs).
*   **Mitigation Actions:** The report details a comprehensive suite of policies:
    *   **Carbon Tax:** Raised five-fold in 2024 to S$25/tCO2e, rising progressively to S$50-S$80/tCO2e by 2030; covers ~80% of national emissions.
    *   **Energy Transition:** Continued reliance on natural gas (~95%), maximizing solar deployment (target ≥2 GWp by 2030), importing low-carbon electricity (target 6 GW by 2035), exploring emerging options (hydrogen, ammonia, geothermal, CCUS, nuclear studies).
    *   **Energy Efficiency:** Key strategy across industry (ECA, grants), buildings (Green Mark, SGBMP '80-80-80' targets, MEPS), households (MELS/MEPS).
    *   **Transport:** Promoting Walk-Cycle-Ride, expanding rail, phasing out internal combustion engine vehicles by 2040, promoting EVs (target 60,000 charging points by 2030), VES scheme.
    *   **Waste/Water:** Target 70% recycling by 2030, Tuas Nexus project (co-digestion).
    *   **F-gases:** Phasing down HFCs in RAC sector (GWP limits, technician certification).
*   **Use of ITMOs (Article 6):** Singapore *intends* to use Internationally Transferred Mitigation Outcomes (ITMOs) to meet its NDC target (projected use of ~2.5 MtCO2e/year), having signed Implementation Agreements with Ghana and Papua New Guinea.

**Support Provided (Chapter 3):**

*   Singapore actively provides **capacity-building support** to fellow developing countries, particularly SIDS and LDCs, primarily through the Singapore Cooperation Programme (SCP), including the Climate Action Package (CAP) and Sustainability Action Package (SAP).
*   Support covers areas like climate adaptation and mitigation, ETF transition, Article 6, water management, disaster risk reduction, green finance, and sustainable development.
*   Singapore collaborates with international partners (e.g., US, Japan, UN agencies) on these initiatives and contributes to international climate science (IPCC support) and finance mobilization efforts (e.g., FAST-P initiative).
*   Singapore does not foresee needing to access technology development and transfer support under Article 10.

In summary, the report presents Singapore's 2022 GHG inventory, demonstrates it is on track to meet its 2030 NDC through a wide range of domestic policies complemented by the intended use of ITMOs, and highlights its significant role in providing capacity-building support internationally.
