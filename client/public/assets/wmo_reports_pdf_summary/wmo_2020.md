The report concludes that 2020 was one of the three warmest years on record, characterized by relentless, accelerating climate change. Despite the economic slowdown due to the COVID-19 pandemic, the primary indicators of climate change continued their negative trend.

### Key Climate Indicators

*   **Global Temperature:** The global mean temperature in 2020 was approximately **1.2°C above pre-industrial levels**. The past six years (2015-2020) have been the six warmest on record. A new record temperature of **38.0°C was recorded north of the Arctic Circle** in Verkhoyansk, Russia.
*   **Greenhouse Gases:** Concentrations of major greenhouse gases (CO₂, CH₄, N₂O) continued to increase. Globally averaged CO₂ levels surpassed 410 parts per million (ppm), and the temporary reduction in emissions due to COVID-19 lockdowns had no discernible impact on atmospheric concentrations.
*   **Oceans:** Ocean heat content reached record levels, and more than 80% of the ocean experienced at least one marine heatwave in 2020. The trend in **sea-level rise is accelerating** due to thermal expansion and melting ice sheets. Ocean acidification is also increasing, diminishing the ocean's capacity to absorb CO₂.
*   **Cryosphere (Ice):** The **Arctic sea-ice extent** was the second lowest on record at its annual minimum. The Greenland and Antarctic ice sheets continued to lose mass at an accelerating rate, contributing to sea-level rise.

### High-Impact Events and Consequences

*   **Extreme Weather:** 2020 saw widespread, high-impact weather events, including:
    *   **Heatwaves, Droughts, and Wildfires:** Major wildfires occurred in the western United States, Australia, and Siberia. Severe drought affected large parts of South America.
    *   **Flooding:** Extensive flooding impacted large parts of Africa and Asia, affecting millions and causing significant casualties.
*   **Tropical Cyclones:** The **2020 North Atlantic hurricane season was the most active on record**, with 30 named storms. Cyclone Amphan was the costliest tropical cyclone on record for the North Indian Ocean, causing billions of dollars in damage in India and Bangladesh.
*   **Humanitarian Impacts:** Weather and climate-related events triggered significant population displacement. In the first half of 2020 alone, approximately **9.8 million people were displaced**, largely due to hydrometeorological hazards.

### Compounded Crises: Climate and COVID-19

The report highlights that the COVID-19 pandemic compounded the effects of extreme weather and climate events. The pandemic disrupted agricultural supply chains, hampered disaster response and evacuation efforts, and exacerbated food insecurity for millions already affected by climate-related disasters like floods, droughts, and locust outbreaks.

In conclusion, the report paints a stark picture of a world where the impacts of climate change are intensifying, threatening the achievement of the UN Sustainable Development Goals and requiring urgent, ambitious, and sustained climate action.
