This report confirms that the world continued its warming trend in 2021, with key climate indicators breaking records and leading to widespread, high-impact weather events that caused severe socioeconomic damage.

### Key Climate Indicators

*   **Greenhouse Gases:** Concentrations of key greenhouse gases—carbon dioxide (CO₂), methane (CH₄), and nitrous oxide (N₂O)—reached new highs in 2020. Levels were 149% (CO₂), 262% (CH₄), and 123% (N₂O) of pre-industrial (1750) levels, respectively, ensuring continued warming for generations.
*   **Global Temperature:** The global mean temperature in 2021 was approximately 1.11°C above the 1850-1900 pre-industrial average. The past seven years (2015–2021) were the seven warmest on record.
*   **Ocean Health:**
    *   **Ocean Heat:** Ocean heat content reached a record high in 2021. The upper 2,000 meters of the ocean continued to warm, a change that is irreversible on centennial to millennial timescales.
    *   **Sea-Level Rise:** Global mean sea level reached a new record high, with the rate of rise more than doubling from 2.1 mm/year (1993-2002) to 4.5 mm/year (2013-2021), primarily due to accelerated ice mass loss.
    *   **Ocean Acidification:** The ocean absorbs around 23% of annual CO₂ emissions, causing it to become more acidic. The current open ocean surface pH is the lowest it has been for at least 26,000 years.
*   **Cryosphere (Ice and Snow):**
    *   **Glaciers and Ice Sheets:** Glaciers continued to lose mass at an accelerated rate. In an unprecedented event, rainfall was recorded for the first time at the summit of the Greenland ice sheet.
    *   **Antarctic Ozone Hole:** The 2021 ozone hole was unusually large and deep, reaching a maximum area of 24.8 million km², driven by a strong and stable polar vortex.

### High-Impact Events of 2021

The year was marked by numerous extreme weather and climate events with devastating consequences:

*   **Heatwaves and Wildfires:** Exceptional heatwaves broke records in western North America (Lytton, Canada reached 49.6°C) and the Mediterranean (Syracuse, Sicily reached 48.8°C), leading to catastrophic wildfires.
*   **Flooding:** Deadly and costly flooding occurred in many regions. Henan province in China suffered economic losses of US$17.7 billion, while Western Europe experienced its most severe flooding on record, with losses in Germany exceeding US$20 billion.
*   **Drought:** Severe drought affected many parts of the world, including North America, South America, and Southwest Asia. In the US, Lake Mead fell to its lowest level on record, threatening water supplies for millions.
*   **Tropical Cyclones:** Hurricane Ida was the most significant storm of the North Atlantic season, causing an estimated US$75 billion in economic losses in the United States and becoming one of the costliest hurricanes on record.

### Socioeconomic and Humanitarian Impacts

*   **Food Security:** The combined effects of conflict, extreme weather events, and economic shocks, all exacerbated by the COVID-19 pandemic, reversed decades of progress in fighting hunger.
*   **Population Displacement:** Weather-related disasters continued to be a major driver of internal displacement. As of October 2021, the countries with the highest numbers of new displacements were China (over 1.4 million), Viet Nam (over 664,000), and the Philippines (over 600,000).

In conclusion, the 2021 report paints a stark picture of a planet where climate change is not a future threat but a present and escalating reality, with measurable and damaging impacts on ecosystems and human well-being across the globe.
