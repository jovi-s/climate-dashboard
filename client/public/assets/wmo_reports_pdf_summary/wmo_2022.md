### Summary of the State of the Global Climate 2022

This report from the World Meteorological Organization (WMO) confirms that the signs and impacts of climate change are becoming more dramatic. The year 2022 was marked by record-breaking climate indicators and extreme weather events that caused widespread damage and affected millions of people worldwide.

#### **Key Global Climate Indicators**

*   **Temperature:** The global mean temperature in 2022 was **1.15°C above the pre-industrial (1850-1900) average**. The years 2015-2022 were the eight warmest on record, with 2022 ranking as the 5th or 6th warmest, despite the cooling influence of a multi-year La Niña event.
*   **Greenhouse Gases:** Concentrations of the three main greenhouse gases—carbon dioxide (CO₂), methane, and nitrous oxide—reached **record highs** in 2021 (the latest year for consolidated data). The annual increase in methane was the highest ever recorded. Real-time data shows these levels continued to rise in 2022.
*   **Ocean Heat:** Ocean heat content reached a **new record high in 2022**. Around 90% of the excess energy trapped by greenhouse gases is absorbed by the ocean, fueling marine heatwaves. In 2022, 58% of the ocean surface experienced at least one marine heatwave.
*   **Sea Level Rise:** Global mean sea level continued to rise, reaching a **new record high**. The rate of sea level rise has **doubled** since the first decade of satellite measurement (1993-2002).
*   **Cryosphere (Ice):**
    *   **Glaciers:** Experienced extreme melting, with an average thickness loss of over 1.18 meters. The European Alps were particularly hard-hit, shattering melt records.
    *   **Antarctic Sea Ice:** Dropped to its **lowest extent on record** in February 2022.
    *   **Greenland Ice Sheet:** Lost mass for the 26th consecutive year.

#### **Major Extreme Events and Socioeconomic Impacts of 2022**

Climate change impacts were felt through devastating extreme weather events across the globe:

*   **Drought:**
    *   **East Africa:** Endured its worst drought in 40 years, with five consecutive failed rainy seasons, leading to severe food insecurity for an estimated 37 million people.
    *   **Europe & China:** Suffered from record-breaking summer heatwaves and exceptionally dry conditions, leading to wildfires and significant economic losses. Excess deaths from the heat in Europe exceeded 15,000.
*   **Flooding:**
    *   **Pakistan:** Experienced catastrophic flooding from record-breaking monsoon rains. The disaster affected 33 million people, caused over 1,700 deaths, displaced nearly 8 million, and resulted in an estimated US$30 billion in damages and economic losses.
*   **Population Displacement:** Weather-related disasters, particularly drought and floods, were a major driver of new population displacement. The drought in Somalia internally displaced almost 1.2 million people, while the floods in Pakistan displaced millions more.

In conclusion, the 2022 report paints a clear picture of an accelerating climate crisis. The scientific evidence shows a planet continuing to warm at an alarming rate, with devastating consequences for ecosystems, food security, and human lives, underscoring the urgent need for enhanced climate action and robust early warning systems.
