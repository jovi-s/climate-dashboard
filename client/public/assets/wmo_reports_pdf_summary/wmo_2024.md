### Overall Picture: A Year of Unprecedented Climate Extremes

The World Meteorological Organization's report for 2024 paints a stark picture of a climate system under immense stress. The year shattered numerous records, confirming an accelerated warming trend with devastating consequences across the globe. Nearly every major climate indicator—from temperature and ocean heat to sea-level rise and ice melt—moved further into record-breaking territory.

### Key Findings at a Glance

*   **Warmest Year on Record:** 2024 was the warmest year in the 175-year observational record, with the global average temperature reaching **1.55°C** above pre-industrial levels (1850-1900). The past ten years (2015-2024) are now officially the ten warmest years ever recorded.
*   **Greenhouse Gases at New Highs:** Concentrations of carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide reached their highest levels in at least 800,000 years, continuing their relentless climb.
*   **Oceans in Peril:** The oceans absorbed a record amount of heat, leading to the highest ocean heat content ever measured. The rate of ocean warming over the past two decades is more than double the rate seen from 1960–2005. This fueled marine heatwaves, accelerated sea-level rise, and increased ocean acidification.
*   **Cryosphere Collapse Accelerates:** The planet's frozen regions (the cryosphere) experienced alarming melt rates.
    *   **Glaciers:** Suffered another year of extreme mass loss. The last three years represent the largest negative three-year mass balance on record.
    *   **Sea Ice:** Antarctic sea-ice extent was the second lowest on record for both its annual minimum and maximum. The 18 lowest Arctic sea-ice minimums have all occurred in the last 18 years.
*   **Record Sea-Level Rise:** Global mean sea level reached a new record high. The rate of rise over the last decade is more than double the rate from the first decade of satellite monitoring (1993-2002).

### Driving Factors and High-Impact Events

Several factors contributed to the extreme conditions of 2024:
*   **El Niño:** A strong El Niño event, which peaked at the start of the year, provided a significant, though temporary, boost to global temperatures.
*   **Other Factors:** The report also notes that other elements, such as reduced shipping aerosol emissions and the effects of the 2022 Hunga Tonga volcano, likely contributed to the exceptional warmth.

These conditions fueled extreme weather events worldwide, leading to the highest number of new displacements recorded in a year since 2008. Notable events included:
*   **Tropical Cyclones:** Devastating cyclones like **Yagi** in Asia, **Helene** and **Milton** in the United States, and **Chido** in the Indian Ocean caused widespread destruction and loss of life.
*   **Flooding:** Extreme rainfall caused major flooding in the Sahel region of Africa, Brazil, and Spain.
*   **Drought and Wildfires:** Severe drought affected Southern Africa and large parts of the Americas, leading to food insecurity and destructive wildfires.
*   **Extreme Heat:** Prolonged and intense heatwaves impacted vast regions, with a significant event during the Hajj pilgrimage in Saudi Arabia leading to numerous casualties.

### The Paris Agreement Context

While 2024 surpassed the 1.5°C threshold for a single year, the report clarifies this **does not mean the long-term goal of the Paris Agreement has been breached**. The Agreement refers to a long-term average warming (e.g., over 20 years). However, this "wake-up call" demonstrates that the world is moving perilously close to that long-term limit, underscoring the urgency of climate action.
