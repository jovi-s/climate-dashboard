The World Meteorological Organization’s reports for 2020-2024 paint an unbroken, worsening picture of the climate crisis: every year lands among the warmest ever recorded—with 2024 the hottest in the 145-year instrumental record—and atmospheric CO₂, CH₄ and N₂O all climb to new highs unseen in at least 800,000 years.

Ocean heat content, acidification and de-oxygenation intensify as the seas absorb ever more excess energy, driving faster-than-expected sea-level rise and amplifying powerful El Niño effects. Glaciers and the polar ice sheets continue to shed mass at record rates, Arctic and Antarctic sea-ice hit near-historic lows, and the cryosphere’s decline accelerates global feedback loops. These physical changes fuel escalating extremes—longer heatwaves (with Siberia surpassing 38 °C), megadroughts, catastrophic floods, record North Atlantic hurricane seasons and increasingly destructive cyclones—disrupting food security, displacing millions and inflicting mounting economic losses worldwide.

In short, every key climate indicator is moving in the wrong direction, underscoring the urgent need for rapid, deep cuts in greenhouse-gas emissions and accelerated adaptation efforts.
