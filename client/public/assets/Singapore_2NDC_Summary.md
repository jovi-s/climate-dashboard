**Singapore's Second NDC Target (2035):**

*   Singapore commits to reducing its greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions to between **45 to 50 million tonnes of carbon dioxide equivalent (MtCO2e) in 2035**.
*   This is an **economy-wide, absolute emissions reduction target** for the single year 2035.
*   The implementation timeframe is **1 January 2031 to 31 December 2035**.

**Scope and Coverage:**

*   The target covers all key sectors: Energy, Industrial Processes and Product Use (IPPU), Agriculture, Land Use, Land-Use Change and Forestry (LULUCF), and Waste.
*   It includes the following GHGs: Carbon dioxide (CO2), Methane (CH4), Nitrous oxide (N2O), Hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs), Perfluorocarbons (PFCs), Sulphur hexafluoride (SF6), and Nitrogen trifluoride (NF3).

**Methodology and Accounting:**

*   Singapore will account for emissions and removals using the **2006 IPCC Guidelines** (and subsequent supplements/guidance), employing a Sectoral approach.
*   GHG aggregation will use **100-year Global Warming Potential (GWP) values from the IPCC Fifth Assessment Report**.
*   Progress and achievement will be reported under the Biennial Transparency Report (BTR) and National Inventory Report (NIR) in accordance with the UNFCCC Enhanced Transparency Framework (ETF).

**Planning and Implementation:**

*   The NDC was prepared considering national circumstances, technical studies, stakeholder consultations, outcomes of the **1st Global Stocktake (GST)**, and Singapore's Long-Term Low Emissions Development Strategy (LT-LEDS) aiming for **net zero by 2050**.
*   The **Inter-Ministerial Committee on Climate Change (IMCCC)** leads the whole-of-government effort.
*   Implementation relies heavily on domestic policies:
    *   A **significantly increasing carbon tax** (reaching S$50-S$80/tCO2e by 2030).
    *   Decarbonising the power sector (maximising solar, importing low-carbon electricity, exploring ammonia/geothermal/CCS, studying nuclear).
    *   Improving energy efficiency across sectors (industry, buildings, households).
    *   Reducing transport emissions (Walk-Cycle-Ride strategy, EV adoption, phasing out internal combustion engines).
    *   Phasing down HFCs (Kigali Amendment) and addressing methane.
*   **International Cooperation (Article 6):** Singapore intends to use internationally transferred mitigation outcomes (ITMOs) under Article 6 to meet its target, covering residual emissions after domestic efforts. Cross-border electricity imports and CCS are also key international cooperation elements.

**Fairness, Ambition, and National Context:**

*   Singapore presents the target as ambitious progress beyond its first NDC, despite significant national constraints: being small, low-lying, land-scarce, highly urbanised, alternative energy-disadvantaged, lacking natural resources, dependent on international trade, and vulnerable to climate change.
*   Justification points include early actions (fuel switching, vehicle quotas), strong carbon pricing, decisive actions across sectors, contributions to GST outcomes (renewables, efficiency, fossil fuel transition, coal phase-down), and active international engagement (capacity building via SCP, finance mobilization via FAST-P).
*   Success is contingent on international partnerships (for electricity imports, CCS, ITMOs) and technological maturity.

**Contribution to Global Goals:**

*   The 2035 NDC, coupled with the 2050 net-zero goal, demonstrates commitment to the Paris Agreement's long-term temperature goal and supports the collective effort for global peaking of GHG emissions.

**Adaptation:**

*   Significant adaptation efforts are required and ongoing due to climate vulnerability, but detailed information is provided in Singapore's separate Adaptation Communication.
